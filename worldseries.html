SUMMARY OF TIMELINE:

THE PRIME (1990-1993)

  1. World Series Bound
    • Playoff baseball became a routine in the late 80s and early 90s for the Jays
    • Dave Stieb threw a no-hitter in the 1990 season
    • In 1991, the Jays hosted the all Star game and returned to the Postseason.
    • Unfortunately lost to the Twins in the ALCS again
    • 1992 Season:
      1. Finished with 96 Wins
      2. Attendance record, over 4 million in total attendance
      3. Beat Oakland in the ALCS, making their first World Series
      4. Faced Atlanta in the World Series, with several memories:
        1. Ed Sprague go-ahead Home Run in Game 2
        2. "The Catch" By Devon White keeping the game tied in Game 4
        3. The Double by Dave Winfield in Game 6 to win the World Series
  2. 1993 Season:
    1. Finished with 95 Wins
    2. 3 Jays were in the top 5 in batting average, with all of them top 3
    3. Beat Chicago in ALCS, making their second World Series in 2 years
    4. Faced Philidelphia, where:
      1. Joe Carter's iconic Game 6 Walk-off Home Run sealed the series for Toronto
      2. "Touch em all, Joe"

A GRADUAL DECLINE (1994-2000)

  1. Striked Season (1994)
    • The Jays finished the 1994 season (shortened season) with a 55 and 60 record
    • After 1993, the Jays never returned to the Playoffs again in the 1990s.
  2. A whole lot of Nothing (1995-1999)
    • Nothing happened much for the franchise between 1995-1999
    • Key players like Dave Stieb and Joe Carter retired, and others were traded
    • The losing seasons began piling on
  3. Level of Excellence (1999)
    • Dave Stieb and George Bell were introduced into the Level of Excellence
    • Joe Carter and Cito Gaston were added shortly after
    • Gaston stepped down as Blue Jays Manager in 1997, ending a glorious run
