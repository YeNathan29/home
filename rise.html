THE RISE TO A WORLD SERIES

SUMMARY OF TIMELINE:

THE START OF A NEW CONTENDER (1980-1984)

  1. The goal of a Winning Season (1980-1982)
    • 1980s represented a new phase for the Franchise
    • In 1980-1982, the Blue Jays steadily improved in Wins every season:
      1. 1980: 67 Wins
      2. 1981: 37 Wins (Strike Season)
      3. 1982: 78 Wins
  2. Over .500 (1983)
    • Significant Year for the Jays
    • Marks the start of 11 CONSECUTIVE winning seasons for the Blue Jays
    • First year with a winning Record
    • Record: 89 Wins (Record for the Franchise)
    • Multiple Award Winners:
      • Player of the week Honours:
        1. Luis Leal
        2. Dave Stieb
        3. Lloyd Moseby

PLAYOFF BASEBALL (1985-1990)

  1. A Big Jump (1985)
    • Blue Jays won their first Division Title (First place in Division) in 1985
    • Average fan attendance crossed 30,000 for the first time
    • Made first Playoff Appearance
    • Unfortunately, lost in the Semi-Finals (ALCS) to Kansas City in 7 Games
  2. A change in Scenery (1989)
    • Jays got off to a rough start in 1989 season (12-24 record)
    • Manager Jimy Williams was fired and replaced by Cito Gaston
    • On May 28th, the Jays played their final game in Exhibition Stadium
      • The Jays moved to the Skydome (known as Rogers Centre now)
      • The stadium had an attendance of around 50,000
